David Rach


I am a Molecular Microbiology and Immunology PhD Candidate at the University of Maryland, School of Medicine in Baltimore, Maryland. My thesis advisors are Drs. Cristiana Cairo and Kirsten Lyke.

My thesis project focuses on understanding the impact that prenatal exposure to maternal HIV and/or antiretrovirals has on innate-like and adaptive T cells in HIV-exposed Uninfected (HEU) infants during early life.

I am a graduate of Northwest College receiving my A.S in Natural Resources Biology. It was here that thanks to opportunities to carry out undergraduate research as part of INBRE that I first became interested in Microbiology.

After Northwest, I transferred to the University of Wyoming where I received a B.S. in Molecular Biology and Microbiology, and did undergraduate research on NK cell responses to Toxoplasma gondii infection in the lab of Jason Gigley.

Before moving to Baltimore for graduate school, I served in the Peace Corps as a Secondary Science Teacher in Ghana from 2016-2018.
